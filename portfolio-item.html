This is Clothes Cabin

UX/UI Research

The Problem

We observed that users had a hard time finding Clothes Cabin's hours of operation and there was no clear message about what the organization did to support the local community. These are valid reasons why potential donations were being lost due to how the organization appears to ideal users online.


Project Summary

Through our user and stakeholder interview, my teammates and I discovered that Clothes Cabin official website was not empathizing with our potential users who might want to donate clothes or contribute financially and instead left users feeling indifferent of the organization.

Research Goal & Findings

My team and I planned and conducted 4 total interviews - with 1 of those I had personally conducted with Amanda Nosbisch, the executive director at the non-profit organization. This helped give our design team valid insight on users who were searching to donate to a local charity.

Ideation Design

Once my team and I were clear on our main goal for the redesign, we created our target user persona, sketching out our initial wireframes and deisgn elements, mapping the ideal scenario that users are looking for and that aligns with the goals of the organization, and prioritizing the information structure on the navigation.

Usability Testing

My team and I performed a total of 9 usability tests from low to high fidelity with users having a 100% success rate from our final prototype.


Interactive Prototype & Final Thoughts

Transparency & being clear about their services, hours, etc that the organization offers, Consistent branding that portrays the main “feeling” the organization is giving to its users, Navigation that allows for easy & quick browsing.
